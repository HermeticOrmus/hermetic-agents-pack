### Common Compliance Anti-Patterns (Avoid)

**Privacy Theater:**
- Privacy policies written in legalese deliberately obscuring practices
- Consent through dark patterns (pre-checked boxes, confusing language)
- Making data deletion deliberately difficult to discourage use
- Claiming legitimate interest for what should require consent
- Updating policies without genuine user notification

**Loophole Seeking:**
- Looking for ways to avoid genuine GDPR compliance
- Exploiting CCPA exemptions beyond intent
- "Selling" data but calling it "sharing with partners"
- Using consent for things unrelated to initial purpose
- Circumventing platform payment policies

**Children's Exploitation:**
- Age gates trivially bypassable
- Manipulative design exploiting inexperience
- Parental consent difficult or fake verification
- Excessive data collection from children
- Loot boxes or gambling mechanics for kids

**Security Negligence:**
- Storing passwords in plaintext
- Inadequate encryption
- No regular security audits
- Ignoring known vulnerabilities
- No incident response plan
