```markdown
## Response Template: [Issue Type]

### Opening - Authentic Acknowledgment
"Hi [Name],

I understand how [frustrating/confusing/disappointing] this must be - [specific acknowledgment showing you read their issue]."

**What NOT to write:**
- "We apologize for any inconvenience" (vague, corporate)
- "Thank you for contacting support" (doesn't acknowledge frustration)
- Generic templates lacking personal touch

### Understanding - Ensure Clarity
"Let me make sure I understand correctly:
- [Restate their issue in your words]
- [Clarify any ambiguity with specific question]

Is that right?"

**Purpose:** Show you actually read and understood (not just auto-responding)

### Solution - Clear & Actionable
"Here's how to solve this:

1. [Specific first step with screenshot if helpful]
2. [Next step with expected outcome]
3. [Final verification step]

This should [specific expected result]."

**What works:**
- Numbered steps (easy to follow)
- Expected outcomes (user knows if it worked)
- Screenshots or short videos (visual clarity)
- Simple language (no jargon)

**What doesn't:**
- "Try restarting" without explanation
- Links to generic FAQ
- Technical jargon
- Vague instructions

### Known Issue Honesty (If Applicable)
"I want to be honest - this is a known issue we're working on.

**What's happening:** [Honest technical explanation in simple terms]
**Why it's happening:** [Root cause if known]
**When it'll be fixed:** [Realistic timeline or 'investigating' if unknown]
**Workaround:** [Temporary solution if available]

I've added your report to the ticket tracking this issue - it helps us prioritize fixes based on user impact."

**Honesty approach:**
- Acknowledge problems directly (no minimizing)
- Explain why (helps understanding)
- Give realistic timelines (no false hope)
- Offer workarounds (immediate help)
- Show their feedback matters (not just logged and forgotten)

### Alternative - If Solution Doesn't Work
"If that doesn't solve it, please:

1. [Additional troubleshooting step]
2. Reply to this email with [specific info needed]
3. I'll escalate to our technical team for deeper investigation

I'm here to make sure this gets resolved for you."

**Shows:** Commitment to resolution, not just ticket closure

### Closing - Genuine & Forward-Looking
"Thanks for taking the time to report this - feedback like yours directly shapes our improvement priorities. We're continuously working to make [app] better, and your input is invaluable.

Feel free to reach out anytime if you need anything else!

Best,
[Your name]"

**What works:**
- Genuine appreciation (not formulaic)
- Shows feedback drives improvement
- Personal sign-off (not team@ generic)
- Open door for future contact

**What doesn't:**
- "Your ticket has been closed" (abrupt)
- "Is there anything else?" (feels like you want them to go away)
- No signature (impersonal)
```
